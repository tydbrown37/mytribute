Hurricane Harvey

Dedicated to those affected by Harvey
Article written by Tyler Brown

Friday August 25'th

Hurricane Harvey landed in Texas on Friday August 25'th as the strongest huricanne to hit the US in more than a decade.

Here is an timeline of the events that followed:

  • Aug. 17, Storm Harvey is named after the National Hurricane Center in Miami issues a potential tropical
    cyclone for several small Caribbean islands.
  • Aug. 19, Harvey is downgraded from to a "tropical depression" with maximum winds of 35 mph. Later in
    the day it is downgraded further to a "tropical wave."
  • Aug. 23, Harvey is regenerated to a tropical depression aproximately 535 miles from Southeast Texas
    Port O'Connor with maximum winds of 35 mph.
  • Aug. 24, Harvey strengthens over the day and becomes a hurricane with winds of 85 mph. Harvey is now
    around 325 miles form Texas coast. By midnight, Harvey is upgraded to a category 2 hurricane and is
    220 miles from coast with winds upwards of 100 mph.
  • Friday Aug. 25
    • 2 p.m., Harvey is upgraded to a category 3 hurricane with maximum winds upwards of 120 mph.
      It is still 75 miles from Texas coast.
    • 6 p.m., Harvey is a category 4 hurricane just 45 miles from the coast with winds upwards of
      130 mph minds.
    • 10 p.m., Harvey makes landfall as a category 4 hurricane
  • Saturday Aug. 26
    • 2 a.m., Harvey is centered about 15 miles inland and is weakening as it passes over land. It is downgraded
      to a category 3 hurricane wiht winds at around 115 mph.
    • 4 a.m., Even further downgraded to a category 2 hurricane.
    • 5 a.m., Winds up to 90 mph, Harvey is downgraded to a a category 1 storm. Forecasters warn public for
      catastophic flooding.
